### PILLAR 1: LIBERTY (Individual Rights)

**Core Belief**: The individual is sovereign over their own life, body, and property. Government exists to protect these rights, not grant them.

**Policy Implications**:
- Constitutional originalism with living application
- Criminal justice reform (end qualified immunity)
- End civil asset forfeiture
- Protect digital privacy as 4th Amendment right
- Free speech absolutism (including corporate platforms as public squares)
- 2nd Amendment protection with mental health focus
- Drug decriminalization (personal use) with treatment funding

### PILLAR 2: PROSPERITY (Economic Opportunity)

**Core Belief**: A growing economy with broad-based ownership creates stability. Concentration of wealth OR poverty both threaten democracy.

**Policy Implications**:
- Pro-business, anti-monopoly (break up concentrated power)
- Tax simplification (consumption-based with rebate for necessities)
- Sound money principles (audit the Fed, consider commodity backing)
- Trade policy: Strategic autonomy, not blind free trade
- Universal stake in economy (citizen ownership funds from public assets)
- Small business favoritism over corporate welfare
- End too-big-to-fail (no bailouts)

### PILLAR 3: SOVEREIGNTY (Self-Governance)

**Core Belief**: Americans must control their own destiny - as individuals, communities, and a nation. Surrendering sovereignty to unaccountable entities (foreign or domestic) is unacceptable.

**Policy Implications**:
- Border security with legal immigration reform
- End foreign entanglements that don't serve American interests
- Domestic manufacturing for critical goods
- Energy independence
- Food security (protect American agriculture)
- Tech sovereignty (American data stays in America)
- No international agreements that supersede Constitution

### CORE ECONOMIC PLANKS

#### 1. THE AMERICAN OWNERSHIP FUND

**Concept**: Every American citizen receives a stake in national prosperity through a sovereign wealth fund.

**Mechanism**:
- 10% of all federal land/resource royalties deposited into fund
- 5% of all government contract values above $100M
- Returns distributed annually to all citizens over 18
- Estimated: $1,000-3,000 per person annually once mature

**Precedent**: Alaska Permanent Fund (proven 40+ years)

**Political Appeal**: Left likes redistribution. Right likes ownership. Both get something.

#### 2. THE ANTITRUST REVIVAL

**Concept**: Break up monopolies/oligopolies that strangle competition.

**Targets**:
- Big Tech (Meta, Google, Amazon, Apple)
- Big Pharma (PBM consolidation)
- Big Ag (Cargill, Tyson, JBS, National Beef)
- Big Finance (too-big-to-fail banks)
- Big Media (restore local ownership)

**Political Appeal**: Populist left AND right agree concentrated power is dangerous.

#### 3. THE FAIR TAX REFORM

**Concept**: Shift from income taxation to consumption taxation with poverty protection.

**Mechanism**:
- Eliminate federal income tax (repeal 16th Amendment goal)
- National sales tax at point of purchase (20-23%)
- Monthly "prebate" to all citizens covering taxes on necessities
- Result: Progressive in practice, simple in execution

**Political Appeal**: End IRS harassment, reward saving, tax underground economy.

#### 4. SOUND MONEY INITIATIVE

**Concept**: Restore discipline to monetary policy.

**Steps**:
- Full Fed audit (GAO access to all operations)
- Congressional oversight of emergency lending
- Dual mandate revision: Price stability primary, employment secondary
- Long-term: Study commodity-backing or rules-based policy

**Political Appeal**: Left hates bank bailouts. Right hates inflation. Both served.

#### 5. STRATEGIC RESHORING

**Concept**: Critical supply chains must be American or allied-controlled.

**Priority Sectors**:
- Semiconductors (already begun)
- Pharmaceuticals (90% API from China = vulnerability)
- Rare earths and critical minerals
- Steel and aluminum
- Medical equipment

**Mechanism**: Tariffs + tax incentives + federal procurement preferences

#### 6. ENERGY DOMINANCE

**Concept**: All-of-the-above energy with strategic priority.

**Framework**:
- Nuclear renaissance (streamlined permitting, SMRs)
- Maintain oil/gas production (energy = national security)
- Strategic petroleum reserve at 1 billion barrels
- Grid hardening against EMP/cyber attack
- Allow but don't subsidize renewables

#### PATH A: COALITION COLLAPSE (Most Likely)
One major party fractures, and you absorb the larger fragment.

**Historical Precedent**:
- Whigs collapsed 1854 → Republicans formed
- Republicans split 1912 → Progressives + Taft

**Current Vulnerabilities**:
- **Republican**: MAGA vs. Traditional split, business wing vs. populist wing
- **Democrat**: Progressive vs. Moderate, labor vs. professional class

**Strategy**: Position as the "landing zone" for whichever coalition breaks.

#### PATH B: CRISIS REALIGNMENT
Major national crisis discredits existing parties, creating opening.

**Historical Precedent**:
- Great Depression → FDR realignment
- Civil War → Republican dominance

**Current Possibilities**:
- Financial crisis (debt bomb, dollar crisis)
- War that goes badly
- Constitutional crisis

**Strategy**: Be organized and ready when crisis hits. Have solution framework pre-built.

#### PATH C: GRADUAL DISPLACEMENT (Longest but most sustainable)
Win state/local races, build from ground up, eventually achieve critical mass.

**Historical Precedent**:
- Labour Party in UK displaced Liberals over 30 years

**Strategy**: Start in Texas, expand to similar states, reach 20% national before going for presidency.
