## METHODOLOGY: 4-TEST BOTTLENECK FRAMEWORK

### Test 1: Forced Buyers (Critical)
**Question:** Who MUST buy this asset/commodity regardless of price?

**Criteria:**
- Regulatory requirements (utilities must buy uranium for reactors)
- Operational necessity (airlines must buy jet fuel)
- Supply chain dependency (chip makers must buy rare earths)
- Contractual obligations (delivery commitments)

**Conviction Threshold:** 3+ forced buyer categories identified

---

### Test 2: Supply Constraint (Critical)
**Question:** Is supply physically or structurally limited?

**Criteria:**
- Physical scarcity (limited mines, refineries, production)
- Geopolitical disruption (Strait of Hormuz closure)
- Long restart times (Qatalum 6-12 months)
- Regulatory barriers (permitting delays)

**Conviction Threshold:** Supply cannot increase for 3+ months

---

### Test 3: Irreplaceable (Validation)
**Question:** Can buyers substitute to alternative?

**Criteria:**
- No viable alternatives exist (uranium for nuclear plants)
- Switching costs prohibitive (retooling factories)
- Technical specifications unique (aerospace-grade materials)
- Infrastructure locked-in (natural gas pipelines)

**Conviction Threshold:** Substitution impossible or >6 months timeline

---

### Test 4: Pricing Power (Amplifier)
**Question:** Can sellers raise prices without demand destruction?

**Criteria:**
- Inelastic demand curve (necessities, not luxuries)
- Cost is small % of buyer's total (fertilizer vs farm revenue)
- Alternative more expensive (drilling new wells vs buying oil)
- Time-sensitive needs (spring planting deadlines)

**Conviction Threshold:** Price elasticity <0.5
