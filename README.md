# LaKel

**Hardware-Focused Investment Fund**

*"Capital flows to those who build the physical foundation of the future."*

---

## Overview

LaKel is an investment fund specializing in hardware and deep-tech companies. We identify and invest in companies building the physical infrastructure that enables technological progress—semiconductors, power systems, critical materials, manufacturing, and defense technology.

## Investment Thesis

### The Physical Foundation

While software captures attention, hardware captures value. Every AI model needs:
- **Chips** to run on
- **Power** to operate
- **Cooling** to sustain
- **Materials** to manufacture
- **Infrastructure** to deploy

LaKel invests at the intersection of scarcity and necessity.

### Four Pillars

1. **Bottleneck Thesis**: Companies that control supply-constrained, irreplaceable components
2. **Forced Buyer Dynamics**: Industries where customers MUST purchase regardless of price
3. **Physical Moats**: Manufacturing expertise, regulatory licenses, and resource access that can't be replicated in code
4. **Critical Infrastructure**: Power, grid, defense, and materials that nations depend on

## Focus Sectors

| Sector | Thesis |
|--------|--------|
| **Semiconductors** | Foundry, packaging, equipment, materials |
| **Power Infrastructure** | Grid modernization, transformers, utilities |
| **Nuclear & Uranium** | Fuel cycle, SMR technology, enrichment |
| **Critical Materials** | Rare earths, antimony, lithium, copper |
| **Defense Technology** | Autonomous systems, space, cyber, drones |
| **Manufacturing** | Reshoring, automation, precision equipment |

## Philosophy

> "The crowd chases software margins. We own what software depends on."

Hardware investing requires:
- **Patience**: Physical moats take years to build
- **Technical depth**: Understanding manufacturing, not just markets
- **Contrarian positioning**: Buying when hardware is "boring"
- **Supply chain vision**: Seeing bottlenecks before they bind

## Structure

LaKel operates as an independent investment vehicle, occasionally collaborating with aligned partners on hardware R&D and deep-tech initiatives.
