# LaKel Investment Framework

## The Bottleneck Test

Before any investment, every opportunity must pass the **Four Tests**:

### 1. Forced Buyer Test
> "Does the customer HAVE to buy this, regardless of price?"

- Defense contractors who need specific components
- Utilities who must upgrade aging infrastructure
- Chipmakers who need specific equipment (ASML, LAM)
- Nuclear plants that require specific fuel (HALEU)

**Pass**: Customer has no alternative supplier or substitute product
**Fail**: Customer can delay, switch suppliers, or use alternatives

### 2. Supply Constraint Test
> "Is supply genuinely limited, not just temporarily tight?"

- Physical resource constraints (rare earths, uranium deposits)
- Manufacturing capacity that takes 3+ years to build
- Regulatory/licensing barriers (nuclear, defense)
- Technical expertise concentrated in few players

**Pass**: Supply cannot scale quickly even with unlimited capital
**Fail**: Supply can expand with investment within 12-18 months

### 3. Irreplaceability Test
> "Can this be substituted or engineered around?"

- Antimony in ammunition (no substitute at scale)
- EUV lithography (no alternative technology)
- HALEU for advanced reactors (physics constraint)
- Grid transformers (long lead times, specialized)

**Pass**: No viable substitute exists at required scale/performance
**Fail**: Substitutes exist or are in development

### 4. Pricing Power Test
> "Can the company raise prices without losing customers?"

- Customers accept 2-3x price increases
- Contracts protect margins during commodity swings
- Switching costs exceed price premium
- Market share stable despite price increases

**Pass**: Demonstrated ability to raise prices and maintain volume
**Fail**: Price-sensitive customers, commodity dynamics

## Position Sizing Framework

### Conviction Tiers

| Tier | Allocation | Criteria |
|------|------------|----------|
| **Core (Tier 1)** | 5-10% | Passes all 4 tests, immediate catalyst |
| **Position (Tier 2)** | 2-5% | Passes 3+ tests, thesis developing |
| **Tracking (Tier 3)** | 1-2% | Passes 2+ tests, watching for entry |
| **Research** | 0% | Interesting but not actionable yet |

### Entry Discipline

- **Technical**: RSI < 40 preferred, avoid overbought entries
- **Fundamental**: Catalyst within 6 months
- **Risk**: Define stop-loss before entry (typically -15% to -20%)
- **Size**: Scale in over 2-3 entries, not all-at-once

## Risk Framework

### Portfolio Constraints

| Metric | Target | Hard Limit |
|--------|--------|------------|
| Single Position | 5% | 10% |
| Single Sector | 25% | 35% |
| Cash Reserve | 10% | 5% minimum |
| Leverage | 0% | 0% |

### Scenario Planning

For each position, define:
1. **Bull case**: What happens if thesis plays out? (+50-100%)
2. **Base case**: Moderate success (+20-40%)
3. **Bear case**: Thesis fails or delayed (-20-40%)
4. **Catastrophic**: Black swan event (-50%+)
